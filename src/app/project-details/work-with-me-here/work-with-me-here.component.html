Work With Me Here is a short game about working from home while trying to keep your excited dog at bay; takes place during the 2020 pandemic lockdown. It was created during a game jam by Team Toffee (named after the dog), of which I was the programmer. It was made with the Unity game engine and written in C#. Play Now

  • Simple Controls - Point and click to do work, change rooms, and play with Toffee.
  • Challenging Gameplay - Keep Toffee from chewing on the wires, and unplugging the router while keeping up with emails, writing documents, and answering phone calls, in three difficulty settings.
  • Leaderboard - Submit your name to the leaderboard to see how you stack up against other players.
  • Mobile Support - The game is fully playable on mobile devices.

Joining the GMTK 2020 game jam, I was excited to meet people with different skills and have fun making something interesting. When the topic of the game jam was announced as “out of control”, my team brainstormed to come up with an idea that would be unique and enjoyable.

We eventually settled on creating a game where your pet is out of control while you are working from home. It was a great experience to work with talented people and make something creative while having fun at the same time.

As our 48 hours dwindled, we realized the features we originally wanted to add were too large in scope for the time we had. We had a photo finish with a game stripped down of most of our idea, tied together with duct tape and string using Game Maker 2. It works, just don't look at the code!

A few months passed, and I decided to remaster the game using the Unity engine for a year-end game jam, Indie Lift 2020.

While taking my time to remaster the game, I learned a lot about coding, and how to design software with users in mind. Here's what I learned along the way:

  • Explaining complex concepts, like how to play a game, is a lot harder than it seems unless you
    • assume the reader will skim and plan accordingly
    • use motion to capture attention
    • group related items together using colors and proximity
    • break down complex concepts into chunks so it's easier to digest
    • use visual cues to help the reader understand
  • The only way to code fast is to code well, write code like well-written prose to avoid confusion while collaborating with others.
  • Commit code to source control frequently. I learned this the hard way!
